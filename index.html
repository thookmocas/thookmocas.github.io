Statistics 641 - Statistical Methods for Clinical Trials

University of Wisconsin-Madison

Overview:

A clinical trial is prospective study evaluating the effect of an intervention in humans. Randomized controlled, clinical trials (RCTs) are considered the “gold standard” for assessing comparative safety and efficacy of proposed interventions. To be scientifically convincing, a trial must be properly designed, conducted and analyzed. The goal of this course is to provide students with a basic understanding of the statistical concepts important in the design, conduct and analysis of clinical trials.
